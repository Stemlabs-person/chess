    📖 The Rule Book

    Everything you need to know to play chess — in plain, simple words!

    🎯 The Goal

    Chess is a game for two players. Each player has an army of pieces. Your job is to trap the other player's king so it can't escape — that's called checkmate, and it wins the game!

    🏁 Setting Up

    Each player starts with 16 pieces: 1 king, 1 queen, 2 rooks, 2 bishops, 2 knights, and 8 pawns. White always moves first, then players take turns — one move each.

    ♟️ How Each Piece Moves

    ♙PawnMoves straight ahead 1 square (2 on its very first move). Captures diagonally, one square.
    ♘KnightMoves in an "L" shape — 2 squares one way, then 1 square sideways. It can jump over pieces!
    ♗BishopSlides diagonally, as far as it wants. Stays on one color forever.
    ♖RookSlides in straight lines — up, down, left, or right — as far as it wants.
    ♕QueenThe strongest piece! Moves like a rook and a bishop combined.
    ♔KingMoves just 1 square in any direction. Protect him — he's the most important piece!

    ⚠️ Check, Checkmate & Stalemate

    • Check — the king is under attack! That player must move out of danger right away.
    • Checkmate — the king is under attack AND has no way to escape. Game over — that player loses!
    • Stalemate — the player to move has no legal moves, but their king is NOT in check. The game ends in a draw (nobody wins).

    ✨ Three Special Moves

    • Castling — the king hops 2 squares toward a rook, and the rook jumps to the other side. Keeps the king safe! You can only do this if neither piece has moved yet, and the king isn't in check.
    • En Passant — a sneaky pawn trick! If an enemy pawn zooms past yours by moving 2 squares, you can capture it as if it only moved 1 — but only right away, on your very next move.
    • Promotion — if a pawn reaches the very last row, it transforms into any piece you want (usually a queen)!

    🤝 Good Chess Manners

    • Once you let go of a piece, that move counts — think before you move!
    • Take turns and be patient while your friend is thinking.
    • Say "good game" at the end, win or lose. It's just a game — have fun!
